Neuro Adaptive Material

This project introduces a neuro-adaptive material that replaces rigid gears with a flexible silicone system embedded with magnetic particles and graphene. By using electrical signals to trigger magnetic alignment and stiffness tuning, the material can move and adapt its density without external motors. This creates a lightweight, biomimetic actuator designed for more natural and responsive soft robotics and prosthetics.

Bio-Compatible Scaffolds for Medical Applications

Team Metamorph project transforms eggshell waste into a high-tech bioactive scaffold designed to heal large bone defects by mimicking the natural bone matrix. By combining eggshell-derived nano-Hydroxyapatite with a Gelatin-Chitosan matrix using freeze-drying techniques, the team created a porous 3D structure that promotes cell growth and vascularization. This "waste-to-wealth" approach slashes mineral costs by 90%, offering a sustainable, scalable, and cost-effective solution for advanced orthopedic regeneration.

Satellite-Based Parametric Insurance System using Google Earth Engine for Automated Drought Assessment

This project proposes a Parametric Insurance Model that uses Google Earth Engine to automate risk assessments and payouts for smallholder farmers facing climate-driven droughts. By monitoring Sentinel-2 satellite imagery and rainfall data in real-time, the system triggers automatic financial relief whenever environmental triggers, like NDVI or precipitation, fall below pre-set thresholds. This data-driven approach eliminates the need for manual field inspections, significantly reducing fraud and ensuring immediate payouts to help farmers sustain their livelihoods.

ELECTROSTATIC FOG CONDENSER IN ORDER TO REPLACE THE CONVENTIONAL NET FOR COLLECTING WATER

This project introduces a portable Electrostatic Fog Condenser that uses a 50,000-volt field to actively pull water droplets from the air, overcoming the limitations of wind-dependent passive nets. The solar-powered system features modular chevron-shaped wings that maximize surface area and utilize ionic thrust to collect water even in zero-wind conditions. By combining high-voltage attraction with a sustainable charging cycle, it provides a highly efficient and autonomous water source for arid or foggy environments.
